The Birth of the Web

In 1989, Sir Tim Berners-Lee, a British scientist at CERN, invented the World Wide Web (WWW). His vision was to create a universal information system that allowed scientists and people worldwide to share knowledge seamlessly.

Fun Facts

  • The first website was info.cern.ch, launched in 1991.
  • Tim Berners-Lee also created the first web browser, called WorldWideWeb.
  • The web was initially developed for researchers before becoming public in 1993.
